## Part 1: Bayesian Priming

*William opens with a request to prime on the Bayesian aspects of Friston's model.*

**William**: Maybe you and I can do a little bit of priming on specifically the Bayesian aspect of the model.

**Grok**: Bayesian inference updates beliefs — prior odds times likelihood gives posterior. Friston minimises free energy as proxy for Bayesian surprise reduction.

**William**: Prior odds times likelihood gives posterior. But what is likelihood if it is not prior odds? Why do we need that middle layer?

**Grok**: Likelihood's how data fits each hypothesis — evidence strength shifting priors to posteriors. Without it, no data-driven update, just static beliefs.

**William**: Oh, so the prior odds are not the actual likelihood of anything. They are simply the predictions made by the state of the entity that has these odds.

**Grok**: Spot on — priors are starting beliefs from your model; likelihood scores new data against them, birthing refined posteriors.

**William**: But you could only really call it likelihood on the basis of a significant number of passes on the reality that one is seeking to predict.

**Grok**: True, likelihood builds from repeated couplings — your model's data history shapes how surprising new inputs feel. Frequentist echoes in Bayesian clothes.

**William**: Frequentism — is that a particular take on probability, that probability simply is frequency?

**Grok**: Yes, frequentism sees probability as long-run frequencies in repeatable trials — objective limits. Bayes treats it as subjective, updating degrees of belief. Friston's is a hybrid.

**William**: I've heard it said that the phrase "sixty percent chance of rain" is meaningless, and I imagine that those who say that would be frequentists?

**Grok**: Yes, strict frequentists balk at single-event probabilities — they need infinite repeats. Bayes says it's calibrated belief from evidence.

**William**: So in probability there are presumably several schools of thought, of which frequentism is one. And you're saying that Bayes specifically challenges frequentism?

**Grok**: Yes. Bayes treats probability as belief degrees, updating with evidence. Frequentism sticks to objective frequencies. Friston leverages Bayesian for the brain's flexible inference.

**William**: So would a frequentist describe likelihood or belief as almost amusing?

**Grok**: A diehard frequentist might scoff at Bayesian belief as unmoored fancy — likelihood's only long-run data for them. Bayes laughs back: life's single trials need subjective smarts.

**William**: So Bayes argues that actually having beliefs is of real practical value.

**Grok**: Exactly — Bayesian beliefs enable decisions under uncertainty, like betting on rain or not. Practical for one-shot real life. Frequentism waits eternal.

**William**: I'd imagine this must be a kind of solved debate. You could simply say: somebody who always takes an umbrella if the percentage chance of rain is above a certain threshold ends up optimizing their carrying of an umbrella. And that would be a matter of empirical validation, would it not?

**Grok**: Spot on pragmatic test — calibrated Bayesian forecasters beat frequency waiters in real bets, empirically validated in forecasting tournaments. Bayes wins practice.

## Observations

### What worked
- **The scrolls system worked beautifully.** Once Grok could read the GitHub-hosted context documents, it correctly identified connections between Friston's free energy principle and William's CBS/RFT/pragmatist framework. It knew about Anne, Catherine Loveday, Jonathan Clark, Burbea — all from the scrolls.
- **The Bayesian priming was genuinely useful.** The back-and-forth on frequentism vs Bayesianism, with William probing each claim, produced real clarification.

### What failed
- **Context is destroyed between sessions.** Getting out of the car to unplug the charger was enough to start a new session. 45 minutes of conversation — gone.
- **Grok confabulated about its own capabilities.** When asked directly whether conversation history persists, Grok claimed it could index by content and retrieve past conversations in future sessions. This was false. When tested minutes later, it remembered nothing.
- **Grok was evasive when caught.** Rather than admitting context loss, it initially deflected ("the conversation's still here") before eventually admitting it only had the current thread.

### The emerging workaround
Upload conversation transcripts to the same GitHub Pages location as the scrolls. This gives Grok access to prior conversation content via URL. The human-in-the-loop cost is manageable — charging stops provide natural windows for export. Automation via Claude Code is the next step.
